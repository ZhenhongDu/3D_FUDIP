Abstract

The fluorescent images usually suffer from deterioration introduced by blurring and noise. Deconvolution techniques partially reverse the image degradation, but are susceptible to noise. To break the trade-off between noise suppression and fine detail preservation, we develop a method to fully decouple the deconvolution process into deblurring and denoising parts. Our method, 3D fully uncoupled deconvolution method with alternating iteration based on Poisson model (3D-FUDIP), achieves a nearly 8-fold improvement in spatial resolution. By the employment of Poisson model, 3D-FUDIP can be applied to various noise conditions, especially low-light conditions where the photon number is extremely small, with improvements of signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) by up to 6-fold. Besides, 3D-FUDIP boosts the spatial-bandwidth product (SBP) by one order of magnitude, allowing more spine details to be resolved within larger imaging volume, to some extent circumventing the trade-off between high resolution and wide imaging field of view (FOV). By synergizing deep learning with these advances, we then propose CascadeDeconNet based on 3D-FUDIP to further improve the imaging resolution. Finally, we extend our 3D-FUDIP to various imaging systems, including confocal, two-photon, and light-sheet microscopes, showing the compatibility and potential applications in biological science.
